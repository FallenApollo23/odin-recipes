Garlic Herb Crusted Pork Sirloin Roast

Description

When I was young I had never had good pork. It was always overdone and underseasoned. Now, I didn't know this at the time, I just thought that was how pork was supposed to be. This is the first pork recipe I made on my own and good lord is it one of the best dishes I have ever made. This dish literally changed my entire view on pork being a viable meal. Something that is not only amazingly good but also surprisingly cheap! Without further ado, let's get into it.

Ingredients

  • 2-½ to 3 pound boneless pork roast, pork sirloin
For the rub:
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil

  • 2 to 3 teaspoons fresh garlic, pressed or finely minced

  • 1-½ teaspoons kosher salt

  • ¾ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

  • 1-½ teaspoons fresh sage leaves, finely chopped

  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves, finely chopped

  • 2 teaspoons freshly grated orange zest

Directions

  1. Remove the roast from the regerator 30 minutes ahead of time to bring it to room termperature.

  2. Preheat the oven to 350℉.

  3. Make the rub by combining the olive oil, garlic, salt, pepper, sage, rosemary, thyme, and orang zest in a small bowl.

  4. Using your hands, coat the roast completely with the prepared rub.

  5. Place on a rack in a roasting pan and pour ½-inch of water into the bottom of the pan (this creates moist heat)

  6. Roast the pork until a thermometer inserted into the center of the roast registers between 140℉ and 145℉, 50 to 60 minutes (see notes).

  7. Remove from the oven, tent loosely with foil, and allow the roast to rest for 15 minutes before carving.

  8. Slice and serve on a platter garnished with sprigs of fresh herbs.

Nutrition Facts:

315 calories; 1g carbohydrates; 43g protein;15g fat; 4g saturated fat; 10g polyunsaturated fat; 119mg cholesterol; 326 mg sodium.
